INX FY-2026 Operating Plan

Prepared by: Shreyash Sinha (Founder)
(Execution & Validation Year)

1. Strategic Context

INX Technologies is structured as a layered ecosystem.
FY2026 focuses exclusively on validating and stabilizing the first execution layer:

INX Automations (IA).

This year is not about expansion into new verticals.
It is about building operational credibility.

2. Execution Objective for FY-2026

The sole priority for FY-2026:
Build a stable, predictable automation revenue engine capable of funding future expansion.

All decisions during this period are filtered through one question:
Does this increase revenue reliability and system maturity?
If not, it is deprioritized.

3. Market Focus

Geographic Focus
Patna, Bihar — local market concentration before expansion.

Customer Priority

  • Coaching Institutes
  • Businesses (automation-focused)
  • Static Website Clients

Automation remains the core product.
Websites function as entry leverage and trust accelerators.

4. Current Baseline (Start of FY-2026)

  • Revenue: –₹116.82 (initial domain cost)
  • Current Paying Clients: 0
  • Pipeline: 1 warm institutional lead
  • Capital: Extremely limited working liquidity
  • Founder Structure: Solo
  • operator Structure: Solo

Infrastructure

  • Self-hosted backend
  • Cloudflare Tunnel exposure
  • Active Backend endpoint: • NOT FOR PUBLIC ACCESS •
  • VPS migration planned after second client closure

This baseline reflects early-stage validation status.

5. Product Structure

Core Offering: Automation Systems

Basic Automation

  • Form → Google Sheets → Owner Notification → Student Confirmation

Advanced Automation

  • AI-assisted formatting layer
  • API usage–based scaling

Secondary Offering: Static Websites

  • Premium static design
  • Optional backend integration
  • Independent website delivery option

Only services currently executable with existing infrastructure are offered.

6. Pricing Framework

Automation

  • Setup: Appx. ₹10,000 (gradual increase over time and will be adjusted accordingly)
  • Maintenance: Starting ₹600/month
    Scales based on API usage and workflow intensity.

Websites

  • ₹7,000 – ₹20,000
    Based on scope and integration depth.

Pricing remains disciplined and structured, not negotiated emotionally.

7. Payment Policy

Automation

  • 45% (will be adjusted accordingly) advance required before build initiation
  • Balance upon deployment readiness

Websites

  • Demo first when agreed by client on a call.
  • Full payment before domain routing and final deployment

No deployment without financial commitment.

8. Delivery Standards

  • Standard timeline: Within 7 days
  • Extended window (high load scenarios): Up to 30 days
  • Delays beyond this threshold trigger client compensation adjustments

Speed is secondary to reliability.

9. FY-2026 Measurable Targets

Client Base

  • 15–20 paying automation clients

Monthly Recurring Revenue

  • ₹1,00,000 – ₹1,50,000

Infrastructure Upgrade

  • VPS migration immediately after second client closure

Operational Capacity

  • Maximum 30 active maintenance clients under solo management

Beyond this threshold requires structural redesign.

10. Sales Engine

Primary channels:

  • Direct calls
  • Structured WhatsApp or Email follow-up
  • Physical meetings for qualified prospects

Pipeline structure:

Call → Demo → Meeting → Close → Advance → Build → Deploy → Final Collection

Sales is consultative, not pressure-driven.

11. Constraints Acknowledged

  • Academic obligations during early FY period
  • Limited capital
  • Solo execution bandwidth
  • Early-stage market credibility

These constraints are recognized and planned around, not ignored.

12. Risk Management

  • No premature hiring
  • No capital expenditure without revenue backing
  • No multi-vertical expansion during validation year
  • No public scaling beyond infrastructure readiness

Operational stability precedes growth.

13. Definition of Success

FY-2026 is successful if:

  • Revenue becomes predictable
  • Infrastructure is VPS-stable
  • 15–20 clients are retained
  • Monthly recurring income can internally fund the next execution layer (IE pilot)

Growth without stability does not qualify as success.

14. Strategic Positioning of FY-2026

This is not a scaling year.
This is not a branding year.
This is not an investment-seeking year.

This is a systems-building year.

Execution over visibility.
Discipline over expansion.
Revenue reliability over ambition.

15. Official Social Handles

FINAL NOTE

FY-2026 is the validation year of INX Automations.

No expansion beyond operational capacity.
No premature diversification.
No capital risk without revenue backing.
No decisions driven by ego or impatience.

Client count is secondary to retention.
Revenue size is secondary to predictability.
Speed is secondary to system stability.

Execution discipline will determine progression to the next layer of INX.

If automation revenue does not become stable and structurally sound, expansion into IE does not proceed.

Progression is earned, not assumed.

This document represents commitment to controlled growth, operational maturity, and long-term durability.
